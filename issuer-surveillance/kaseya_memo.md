# Kaseya — issuer memo

## Bottom line
Kaseya is a high-value watch because it looks like the kind of software credit lenders are often comfortable owning: sticky MSP / IT-management exposure, sponsor backing, and scale. That is exactly why it is useful. If this type of name weakens, it suggests private-credit marks may be too generous across a broader slice of software.

## Why it matters
- sponsor-backed software / IT platform
- large private-credit financing package around the Datto combination
- refinancing / repricing activity can reveal market sentiment
- integration and pricing assumptions may prove more fragile than headline stickiness implies

Public-source work to date points to:
- an original debt package of roughly **$3.7bn** tied to the Datto acquisition financing
- lender involvement reportedly including **Golub, Blackstone, Ares, Oak Hill Advisors, Owl Rock / Blue Owl, and Carlyle**
- a 2025 refinancing effort involving roughly **$4bn** of debt with a meaningful first-lien / second-lien structure

## Why it could hurt BDC unsecured bonds
For unsecured BDC bond investors, Kaseya matters as a **confidence transmission case**. If lenders are forced to refinance at weaker economics or if operating pressure becomes visible, it can undermine the idea that software-service platforms are inherently safe first-lien assets.

## Hidden-mark-risk thesis
The likely blind spot is that sticky software narratives can obscure:
- integration risk
- customer pushback
- more fragile cash generation than headline recurring revenue implies
- slower exit optionality than underwritten

## What to watch
- exact refinancing structure and pricing
- first-lien / second-lien split
- lender turnover from private credit into syndicated markets
- evidence of operating strain or weaker-than-expected deleveraging
- whether sticky MSP / IT-management narratives are masking customer pushback or integration fatigue
- whether the refi lowers financing cost because the credit is healthy or simply because markets are temporarily open

## Current working read
Kaseya is a very useful test of whether lenders are underestimating the downside in **consolidated, sticky-on-paper software platforms**. If this kind of name struggles, it would imply the problem is broader than obviously weak software categories.

## Working judgment
- Hidden mark risk: **4 / 5**
- BDC bond relevance: **4 / 5**
- Recovery fragility: **4 / 5**
