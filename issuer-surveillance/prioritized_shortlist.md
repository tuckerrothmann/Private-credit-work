# Prioritized Shortlist for Deepest Follow-Up

## Priority 1 — do first

### 1) Medallia
Why first:
- Best current live stress signal in software private credit.
- Public lender group is unusually visible.
- Good test case for mark divergence, underwriting drift, and recovery quality.

### 2) Finastra
Why first:
- One of the biggest software private-credit loans on record.
- Size alone makes it systemically useful for this project.
- Refi path and marks matter for broader software-credit sentiment.

### 3) Kaseya
Why first:
- Large and actively discussed private-credit / refi story.
- MSP / IT management software should be sticky, so any weakness would be instructive.
- Consolidation / integration angle could complicate downside.

### 4) Pluralsight
Why first:
- Historical restructuring benchmark.
- Useful to anchor recovery assumptions and sponsor/lender behavior in distress.
- Good comparison point for all newer stress candidates.

## Priority 2 — likely rich next wave

### 5) Coupa
- Large software LBO with public reporting tying in private-credit financing.
- Procurement / spend software is important but not immune to corporate budget pressure.

### 6) Anaplan
- Direct-lender involvement appears publicly documented.
- Planning software is sticky, but valuation can be very growth-sensitive.

### 7) RealPage
- Not the cleanest direct-lending case, but regulatory / litigation overhang makes downside analysis unusually interesting.
- Could become a case where legal risk impairs what would otherwise screen as strong recurring-revenue software.

### 8) Zendesk
- Big take-private supported by private credit per Reuters-republished reporting.
- Customer support software is directly exposed to AI-enabled product pressure.

## What to pull for each deep dive
For each priority issuer, collect:
1. ownership timeline
2. debt / financing timeline
3. evidence of private-credit participation
4. signs of refi, amendments, or marks below par
5. product criticality and churn / switching-cost assessment
6. likely recovery drivers and weak points
7. any public BDC exposure or rating-agency commentary
8. explicit checks across ARCC, BXSL, BCRED, HTGC, OBDC, OTF, GSBD, and GBDC where public information exists

## Lender-angle emphasis
Tucker specifically flagged ARCC, BXSL, BCRED, HTGC, OBDC, OTF, GSBD, and GBDC as high-value platforms to monitor. That should shape the follow-up order because the names most likely to teach us something fastest are the ones where issuer stress can be cross-checked against those lenders' public disclosures or ecosystem commentary.

## My view
If the goal is to find the names most likely to teach Tucker something real about **software-credit downside**, the best sequence is:
**Medallia → Finastra → Kaseya → Pluralsight → Coupa → Anaplan → RealPage → Zendesk**

If the goal is to maximize **public lender-visibility**, the first names I would test against ARCC / BXSL / OBDC / GBDC / GSBD / HTGC-style disclosure channels are:
**Pluralsight → Medallia → Finastra → Kaseya → Anaplan → Coupa**
