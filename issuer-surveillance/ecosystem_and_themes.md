## Emerging theses worth tracking

### Thesis 1: software private-credit risk is increasingly a **dispersion story**, not a simple sector call
The problem is probably not “software bad.” It is that software credit may split sharply between:
- mission-critical systems of record with durable switching costs
- categories vulnerable to AI compression, workflow substitution, or customer build-vs-buy shifts

That argues for much deeper sub-sector discrimination than generic “software exposure” labels in BDC decks.

### Thesis 2: public BDC marks may become one of the best windows into otherwise opaque software-credit stress
Because many private loans do not trade and many borrowers do not disclose much, public BDC marks can become one of the few recurring signals for:
- changing lender sentiment
- valuation gaps across holders
- when a software issuer moves from “watch” to “problem credit”

### Thesis 3: recurring-revenue underwriting may have overstated downside protection
The Medallia / Pluralsight pattern suggests that ARR-based underwriting can help lenders get comfortable upfront, but may not protect them later if:
- customer value perception weakens
- growth slows materially
- interest burden rises faster than operating improvement
- strategic buyers are less aggressive than expected in a distressed sale

### Thesis 4: recovery quality in software is probably more about **franchise continuity** than collateral
For many software issuers, the key recovery question is not lien coverage on hard assets. It is whether lenders can preserve:
- customer retention
- product roadmap credibility
- employee / engineering stability
- channel and implementation partner confidence

That is a different workout logic than in asset-heavy sectors.

### Thesis 5: legal / structural complexity can matter as much as operating weakness
Pluralsight’s liability-management path and RealPage’s regulatory overhang suggest that software recoveries can be impaired by:
- unrestricted subsidiary moves
- IP transfers
- sponsor/lender conflict
- antitrust or litigation clouds
- multi-entity software roll-ups with unclear break-up value

### Thesis 6: the most useful public-credit screen may be the intersection of three traits
The best names to prioritize may be those with:
1. clear sponsor-backed software leverage
2. public evidence of private-credit participation
3. enough public BDC / ratings / market commentary to triangulate marks and lender behavior

On that basis, the strongest current teaching set is still:
**Medallia, Pluralsight, Finastra, Kaseya, Coupa, Anaplan, RealPage, Zendesk, Avalara**
