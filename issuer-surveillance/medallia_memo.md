# Medallia — issuer memo

## One-line thesis
Medallia is the clearest live software private-credit case where **soft-asset recovery risk, lender mark dispersion, and execution pressure** may be telling us more about the broader software-credit book than current headline marks fully reflect.

## What the company does
Medallia sells customer-experience and employee-experience software used to collect feedback, analyze behavior, and improve service / retention outcomes across enterprises. It was easy to finance because it fit the classic sponsor-backed software template:
- recurring revenue
- enterprise customers
- embedded workflows
- analytics / data stickiness narrative
- private-equity owner with a strong software playbook

That same template is what makes the name so useful now. If a company like Medallia starts to wobble, the implication is not just "this borrower is weak." It is that recurring-revenue software underwriting may have offered less downside protection than lenders assumed.

## Why it matters
- large, publicly identified direct-lender group
- software business with limited hard-asset recovery support
- reported markdowns and mark dispersion across lenders
- plausible path from issuer stress to broader software-book skepticism

## Debt / financing timeline
- **August 2021:** Medallia agrees to be acquired by Thoma Bravo in a transaction valuing the company at **$6.4 billion**.
- **August 2021:** Latham says the financing included the then-largest **recurring-revenue unitranche** structure and identifies **Blackstone Alternative Credit Advisors** as lead lender along with credit arms of **Apollo** and **KKR**, plus **Antares**.
- **2025:** public reporting indicates meaningful markdowns on the Medallia loan and visible dispersion in marks across lender vehicles.
- **2026:** Medallia remains the cleanest live watch for whether software private-credit marks are still lagging economic reality.

## Capital-structure relevance
The financing visibility here is unusually strong for private credit. Public sources tie the original acquisition financing to a lender group that matters directly for this project because it bridges issuer analysis to major private-credit platforms.

That matters more than the borrower itself. Medallia is useful because it is a **signal asset**:
- if this credit keeps slipping, what does that say about similar software loans?
- if marks diverge widely, how much confidence should we place in any one lender's carrying value?
- if lenders need patience, amendments, or PIK-style flexibility, where else might that be happening quietly?

## Hidden-mark-risk thesis
The core blind spot is that a recurring-revenue software credit can look acceptable in quarterly marks **long after** exit value, refinancing flexibility, or franchise confidence has begun to erode.

## Why it could be more dangerous than current marks imply
### 1) It is a soft-asset recovery story
If Medallia weakens, lenders are not relying on hard collateral. Recovery depends on preserving:
- customer retention
- product relevance
- sales credibility
- employee / engineering continuity
- buyer appetite in a downside sale

### 2) Software marks can lag business deterioration
The bearish case is not that Medallia is obviously insolvent today. It is that software-credit marks can stay too comfortable while:
- product momentum weakens
- execution issues build
- exit multiples compress
- refinancing flexibility deteriorates

### 3) It is a read-through case for the whole software book
If a flagship recurring-revenue software credit backed by major lenders is marked down materially, markets may ask:
- what else is still marked too generously?
- which BDCs or private-credit funds have similar underwriting?
- how many software credits are closer to amendment or payment-flex behavior than public marks suggest?

## First transmission channel to BDC unsecured bond risk
The first transmission channel is likely **portfolio-mark credibility**, not immediate default:
1. lower marks on visible lender portfolios
2. weaker confidence in software underwriting quality
3. higher perceived risk of future amendments / non-accrual migration
4. wider spreads on BDC unsecured bonds if investors reassess asset quality and NAV durability

## What could show up first if the story worsens
Most likely order of events:
1. additional mark pressure / valuation drift
2. more market commentary about underperformance or weak execution
3. amendment, extension, or capital-structure discussions
4. only later, more overt credit-stress recognition

## What would falsify or weaken the bearish view
The Medallia concern would fade if we saw:
- evidence of durable operating stabilization
- proof that recent markdowns were conservative and sufficient
- a refinancing or liability path that confirms lender confidence rather than forced patience
- signs that customer stickiness and franchise value remain strong enough to support solid recoveries

## Working judgment
- Hidden mark risk: **5 / 5**
- BDC bond relevance: **5 / 5**
- Recovery fragility: **5 / 5**
