# Medallia — issuer memo

## Bottom line
Medallia is the **best live software private-credit watch name** for this project because it combines:
- a large, publicly identified direct-lender group
- a recurring-revenue software business with limited hard-asset recovery support
- reported loan mark pressure
- a plausible case that economic deterioration could still outrun formal mark recognition

For a **BDC unsecured bond** lens, Medallia matters less because one issuer alone will break a lender and more because it can signal:
1. whether large private-credit lenders are being forced to acknowledge software stress more aggressively
2. whether recurring-revenue underwriting was too optimistic on downside protection
3. whether similar software credits may be marked too generously today

## What the business is
Medallia sells customer and employee experience software. It was easy to finance on paper because the business fit a classic sponsor-backed software underwriting template:
- recurring revenue
- enterprise customers
- sticky workflows / embedded analytics
- high-margin software profile
- private-equity owner with a credible software playbook

That same underwriting template is exactly why Medallia matters now. If a name like this starts to wobble, it raises broader questions about software credit assumptions rather than just this company.

## Capital-structure relevance
Public sources tie Medallia's 2021 take-private financing to a roughly **$1.8bn unitranche** supported by a lender group including:
- Blackstone
- Apollo
- KKR credit arms
- Antares

That is unusually valuable for this project because it gives us a real bridge from issuer analysis into lender-platform surveillance.

## Why it could be more dangerous than current marks imply
### 1) It is a soft-asset recovery story
If Medallia weakens, lenders are not relying on hard collateral. Recovery depends on preserving:
- customer retention
- product relevance
- sales credibility
- employee / engineering continuity
- buyer appetite in a downside sale

That makes recovery more fragile than a standard first-lien label might suggest.

### 2) Software marks can lag business deterioration
The central bear thesis is not that Medallia is already obviously insolvent. It is that software-credit marks can stay too comfortable while:
- product momentum weakens
- execution issues build
- exit multiples compress
- refinancing flexibility deteriorates

By the time those pressures become obvious in formal marks, unsecured bond investors may already be repricing lender risk.

### 3) It is a read-through case for the whole software book
Medallia is dangerous partly because it can change how investors think about other names. If a flagship recurring-revenue software credit backed by major lenders is marked down materially, markets may ask:
- what else is sitting near par that should not be?
- which BDCs or private-credit funds have similar underwriting?
- how many software credits are one weak quarter away from amendment or deeper marks?

## Why this matters for BDC unsecured bonds
The transmission channel is mainly:
- lower marks on visible lender portfolios
- weaker confidence in software underwriting quality
- higher perceived risk of future non-accruals or amendment chains
- wider spreads on BDC unsecured bonds if investors think NAV and asset quality are more fragile than reported

Medallia is especially useful because it is **not** just a tiny idiosyncratic borrower. It is a signaling asset.

## What could show up first if the story worsens
Most likely order of events:
1. additional mark pressure / valuation drift
2. more market commentary about underperformance or weak execution
3. amendment / extension / refinancing complexity rather than immediate default
4. only later, more overt credit-stress recognition

That sequence is exactly why this name is valuable for bond surveillance: the pain may emerge gradually but still matter to lender confidence well before a clean restructuring headline.

## What would make the bearish view less compelling
The Medallia worry would fade if we saw:
- evidence of durable operating stabilization
- cleaner proof that recent markdowns were conservative and sufficient
- a refinancing or liability path that confirms lender confidence rather than forced patience
- signs that customer stickiness and franchise value remain intact enough to support stronger recoveries
