# Scoring Framework

## 1) Health score
Score each issuer 1-5 using a weighted qualitative framework.

### Dimensions
- Revenue quality
  - recurring revenue mix
  - retention / churn signals
  - customer concentration
  - pricing power
- Growth / momentum
  - organic growth trajectory
  - bookings / pipeline commentary
  - product relevance / competitive position
- Cash flow / leverage
  - debt load
  - interest burden
  - free cash flow conversion
  - refinancing runway
- Sponsor / capital structure risk
  - PE ownership incentives
  - dividend recap history
  - layering / structural subordination
  - preferred equity / holdco debt
- Downside recovery quality
  - hard assets vs soft assets
  - mission-critical product vs replaceable tool
  - buyer universe in distress
  - evidence from comparable restructurings

### Suggested interpretation
- 5: strong credit profile, resilient in mild/moderate stress
- 4: good but monitor leverage / market shifts
- 3: mixed; could go either way depending on execution / refi path
- 2: vulnerable; weaker recovery likely in downside
- 1: acute stress / probable impairment risk

## 2) Confidence score
How much trust we should place in the call.

### Dimensions
- quantity of source material
- recency of source material
- source diversity
- internal consistency
- ability to infer debt / lender exposure

### Suggested interpretation
- 5: rich filing set + strong triangulation
- 4: multiple recent good sources
- 3: decent evidence, still gaps
- 2: mostly press / fragments
- 1: too little to make a real call

## 3) Red flags to tag
- loan prices / marks below roughly 90 as an initial warning threshold
- loan marks dropping by 4+ points over roughly 2-3 quarters
- slowing ARR with high leverage
- heavy EBITDA add-backs
- refi / amend-and-extend chatter
- sponsor dividend recap behavior
- customer budget pressure
- declining NRR / seat contraction
- executive turnover
- sales execution deterioration
- covenant-lite but economics worsening
- second-lien / preferred / holdco layering
- AI substitution / product compression risk
- cyclical end-market exposure paired with inflexible cost base
- leverage leaving little room for error
- change to PIK or broader payment-flex measures
- change to accrual status / non-accrual risk
- restructuring evidence, including liability-management exercises or amendment chains
