# Software Issuer Watchlist (Initial Public-Source Screen)

## Scope / caveat
This is a **public-source initial screen**, not a lender-position map. The goal is to identify software issuers that look relevant for **private credit downside / recovery work**, especially where sponsor ownership, leverage, recurring-revenue underwriting, AI pressure, refinancing needs, or weak hard-asset backing may matter.

I split names into:
- **Confirmed private-credit / direct-lending signal** = public reporting or financing sources explicitly point to direct lenders / private credit.
- **Likely leveraged sponsor-backed screen name** = clearly relevant software LBO / sponsor-backed issuer, but debt stack still needs confirmation.

## Initial takeaways
1. **The cleanest public case studies are Medallia and Pluralsight.** One is a current mark-down / stress story; the other is a full lender-takeover precedent.
2. **Finastra and Kaseya are the next-most-important software credits to map.** Size alone makes them useful bellwethers.
3. **AI risk matters most in software segments that are easier to unbundle or automate**: contact center, support, analytics/visualization, some workflow tools.
4. **Regulatory / end-market pressure can matter as much as leverage**: RealPage is the best example.
5. **Recovery work should not assume SaaS = good collateral.** In stress, value depends on product criticality, churn behavior, buyer universe, and whether lenders can preserve customer confidence during a workout.
