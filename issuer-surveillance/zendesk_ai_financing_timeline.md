# Zendesk AI / Financing Timeline

## Purpose
Combine the financing history and the category-risk framing so Zendesk can be tracked as both a large software LBO and an AI-sensitive customer-support credit.

## Timeline
- **2022-06-30** — Latham says it advised on financings supporting Zendesk's **$10.2 billion** acquisition by Hellman & Friedman and Permira, including the largest-ever ARR financing and the largest direct-lending leveraged buyout in history at the time.
- **2022-11-22** — Zendesk says the acquisition has closed at approximately **$10.2 billion**.
- **2022 Q4 / 2023** — project research indicates ARCC reported a funded and unfunded first-lien Zendesk position, giving the name direct public-BDC relevance.
- **2026-01-31** — BCRED portfolio materials list **Zendesk, Inc.** as a first-lien senior secured investment.
- **2026 portfolio evidence** — Blue Owl Technology Finance portfolio holdings also list **Zendesk, Inc.** as a first-lien senior secured loan.
- **2023-2026** — the broader investment case shifts toward whether customer-support software is more vulnerable to AI-driven pricing pressure, seat compression, and product substitution than generic software underwriting assumed.

## Why this file matters
Zendesk sits at the intersection of:
- large-scale direct-lending relevance
- recurring-revenue software underwriting
- category-specific AI disruption risk
- cleaner public BDC / vehicle surveillance than many private software names
