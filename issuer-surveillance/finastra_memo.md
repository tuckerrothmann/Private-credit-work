# Finastra — issuer memo

## What the company does
Finastra is a large financial-software vendor serving banks and other financial institutions across core banking, payments, lending, treasury, and capital-markets workflows. In lender terms, it is the kind of company that can look attractive because it sits inside mission-critical customer processes, benefits from recurring software / maintenance revenue, and appears expensive to rip out quickly.

## Bottom line
Finastra is a **bellwether software private-credit refi case**. The key issue is not just whether the company survives; it is whether one of the largest software private-credit loans can refinance cleanly without forcing lenders to accept weaker economics, lower marks, or a more cautious view of the whole software bucket.

## Why it matters
- Vista-backed software issuer
- record-setting private-credit financing size
- meaningful lender syndicate across major platforms
- refinancing activity itself is the signal

Public-source work to date points to:
- a roughly **$5.3bn** 2023 refinancing package
- a documented structure of about **$4.82bn senior secured unitranche term loan + $500mm multicurrency revolver**
- more than **20 lenders / borrowers across multiple jurisdictions** in the original refinancing according to Kirkland's deal note
- lender participation reported elsewhere as including **Oak Hill Advisors, Blue Owl, Ares, HPS, Oaktree, KKR, and Carlyle**
- 2025 refinancing activity that appears to test whether the market will take out part of the private-credit stack on better terms

## Why it could hurt BDC unsecured bonds
If a giant software credit like Finastra needs a more difficult or more expensive refinancing path than expected, it can:
- pressure marks on directly exposed lenders
- worsen sentiment toward similar software credits still marked more optimistically
- raise questions about how easy it really is to exit or refinance big private-credit software positions

## Hidden-mark-risk thesis
The danger is that **size and reputation can delay recognition**. A large, well-known asset may continue to look manageable until the refi process reveals the real market clearing level for risk.

## What to watch
- refinancing terms versus prior debt package
- lender mix changes
- any need for preferred equity or sponsor support
- whether takeout activity validates or challenges prior marks
- whether syndicated markets demand meaningfully different economics than direct lenders accepted
- whether any partial takeout leaves the most difficult risk with the original private lenders

## Current working read
Finastra is dangerous less because it looks like the weakest business in the universe and more because it is a **market-clearing event for software private-credit risk**. If a marquee name of this size cannot refinance cleanly, that could force investors to re-rate a much broader set of software private-credit marks.

## Working judgment
- Hidden mark risk: **4 / 5**
- BDC bond relevance: **5 / 5**
- Recovery fragility: **4 / 5**
