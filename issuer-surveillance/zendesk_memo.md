# Zendesk — issuer memo

## One-line thesis
Zendesk is one of the cleanest **AI-risk software credit** cases in the set: a large, directly financed software LBO where the key question is whether lenders are still giving too much credit to recurring-revenue stickiness in a category that may be compressed by AI faster than marks reflect.

## What the company does
Zendesk provides customer-support and customer-service software, including ticketing, workflow automation, knowledge-base tools, and AI-enabled support products. It has historically looked easy to finance because customer-support systems are often embedded in daily operations and linked to high-volume workflow.

But the category also sits unusually close to AI-driven automation risk, which makes it useful for surveillance. The issue is not that AI automatically breaks the business tomorrow. The issue is that customer-support software may be one of the places where pricing pressure, seat compression, and product substitution appear earlier than lenders expect.

## Why it matters
- large take-private with substantial direct-lending support
- landmark ARR financing and preferred-equity support structure
- customer-support software sits close to AI-enabled workflow change
- potentially useful bridge from software-category disruption into lender marks

## Debt / financing timeline
- **June 2022:** Latham says it advised on financings supporting Zendesk's **$10.2 billion** acquisition by the investor group led by Hellman & Friedman and Permira.
- **June 2022:** Latham describes the package as the **largest ever ARR financing** and says it supported the **largest direct-lending leveraged buyout in history** at the time, along with preferred equity financing.
- **November 2022:** Zendesk says the acquisition closes in an all-cash transaction valuing the company at approximately **$10.2 billion**.
- **2022-2023:** project research and public reporting tie the lender group to major direct lenders including **Blackstone, Apollo, Blue Owl, and HPS**.
- **Q4 2022 / 2023 reporting:** project research also points to at least some public BDC linkage through reported Ares Capital exposure, making the name more relevant for bond surveillance than a generic software screen.

## Capital-structure relevance
Zendesk matters because it is not just a software name in the abstract. It is a very large financed transaction with direct-lending relevance and a category-level question attached to it.

If customer-support software becomes more vulnerable to AI compression than lenders expected, Zendesk could become an early signal that:
- recurring-revenue underwriting was too generic
- software marks are slow to incorporate category-specific disruption risk
- lenders may need to reassess recovery assumptions for customer-experience / support software

## Hidden-mark-risk thesis
Marks may lag because revenue can look stable for a while **before** AI-driven pricing pressure, seat compression, or weaker product differentiation shows up clearly in debt-market risk pricing.

## Why it could matter to BDC unsecured bonds
For BDC bond investors, Zendesk matters because it can reveal that "software" was underwritten too generically. If customer-support software ends up being more vulnerable to AI disruption than lenders assumed, similar marks across visible BDC and private-credit portfolios may need to move.

## First transmission channel to BDC unsecured bond risk
The first transmission channel is likely **category-level software repricing**, via:
1. weaker confidence in customer-support software underwriting
2. lower marks or more cautious carrying values on similar software credits
3. concern that category disruption is not yet reflected in broader software books
4. wider BDC unsecured bond spreads if investors think lender marks lag real risk in AI-sensitive categories

## What to watch
- evidence of AI-driven pricing pressure or seat compression
- whether Zendesk's AI strategy strengthens the moat or simply narrows the gap with peers
- refinancing terms and lender behavior
- any divergence between public product optimism and debt-market risk pricing
- whether public BDC or direct-lender vehicles begin to mark the exposure differently

## What would falsify or weaken the bearish view
The Zendesk concern would soften if we saw:
- durable evidence that AI improves Zendesk's competitiveness rather than compresses it
- healthy pricing and retention despite category disruption
- refinancing terms that validate lender confidence
- little sign of mark pressure despite closer scrutiny of AI-sensitive software credits

## Working judgment
- Hidden mark risk: **4 / 5**
- BDC bond relevance: **4 / 5**
- Recovery fragility: **4 / 5**
