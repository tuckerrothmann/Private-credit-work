# Zendesk — issuer memo

## Bottom line
Zendesk is one of the most interesting **AI-risk software credit** names in the watch set. The concern is not that AI automatically breaks the company tomorrow; it is that customer-support software may be one of the categories where lenders are slow to reprice genuine product and pricing disruption risk.

## Why it matters
- large take-private with substantial private-credit backing
- customer-support software sits in a category exposed to AI-driven workflow change
- recurring revenue may overstate durability if AI changes seat counts, pricing, or competitive moats

Public-source work to date points to:
- roughly **$5bn** of private-credit financing in the buyout
- lender involvement reportedly including **Blackstone, Apollo, Blue Owl, and HPS**
- an unusually useful setup for testing how AI-risk narratives eventually translate into lender marks

## Why it could hurt BDC unsecured bonds
If customer-support software proves more vulnerable to AI compression than lenders expected, marks across similar software exposures may need to move. For BDC unsecured bond investors, that matters because it can reveal that “software” was underwritten too generically.

## Hidden-mark-risk thesis
Marks may lag because:
- revenue can remain superficially stable before deeper product pressure appears
- AI upside narratives can mask the possibility of AI commoditizing part of the offering
- lenders may assume customer-service software remains sticky longer than reality warrants

## What to watch
- evidence of AI-driven pricing pressure or seat compression
- whether Zendesk's AI strategy strengthens moat or just defends against disruption
- refinancing terms and lender behavior
- any gap between public optimism and debt-market risk pricing

## Working judgment
- Hidden mark risk: **4 / 5**
- BDC bond relevance: **4 / 5**
- Recovery fragility: **4 / 5**
