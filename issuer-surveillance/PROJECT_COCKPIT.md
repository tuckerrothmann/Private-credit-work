# Private Credit Issuer Project

## Goal
Build a software-first inventory of sponsor-backed / private-credit-financed issuers where public and semi-public materials are rich enough to form an informed view on credit health, downside risk, and potential recovery quality.

## Investor lens
Primary lens: **BDC unsecured bond investing**.

The practical question is not just which issuer is weak. It is which underlying issuers could create trouble for BDC / private-credit lenders **before that trouble is fully reflected in marks, accrual status, or market perception**.

## Initial thesis
Software issuers may create asymmetric downside for private-credit lenders because they can combine:
- recurring revenue optics
- PE-driven leverage
- EBITDA add-backs / adjusted metrics
- slowing growth or weaker retention hidden beneath headline margin narratives
- limited asset hard collateral and potentially weaker recoveries in downside scenarios

## First workstreams
1. Build issuer inventory
2. Define scoring framework
3. Gather source pack per issuer
4. Form initial health calls
5. Map likely lender / BDC exposure where possible

## Source types
- BDC filings and portfolio schedules
- issuer debt filings / SEC docs where available
- IR decks / lender presentations
- company websites and product pages
- sponsor pages / financing announcements
- press coverage and restructuring / refinancing notes
- rating agency notes where publicly available

## Proposed scores
### Health score (1-5)
1 = distressed / very weak
2 = fragile
3 = mixed / watchlist
4 = solid
5 = strong

### Confidence score (1-5)
1 = sparse, low-quality evidence
2 = limited triangulation
3 = decent picture from multiple sources
4 = strong source coverage
5 = unusually high confidence from rich and current disclosures
