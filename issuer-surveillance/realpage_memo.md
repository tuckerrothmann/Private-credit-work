# RealPage — issuer memo

## Bottom line
RealPage is a **special-situation software credit**. The core issue is that regulatory and litigation overhang can impair enterprise value even if recurring revenue and software-like margins initially make the credit look financeable.

## Why it matters
- large Thoma Bravo-backed software LBO
- meaningful first-lien / second-lien debt structure publicly referenced
- regulatory / antitrust pressure tied to rent-setting software
- valuation damage can arrive through legal overhang before classic operating collapse

Public-source work to date points to:
- roughly **$3bn** initial first-lien financing plus about **$1bn** second-lien financing around the buyout
- later capital-structure references showing a still-large first-lien / second-lien stack
- a regulatory and litigation cloud that can impair lender recoveries indirectly by shrinking buyer appetite and valuation confidence

## Why it could hurt BDC unsecured bonds
RealPage matters less as a pure software-operations story and more as a reminder that **legal / regulatory shocks can make marks stale**. If a lender book contains similar “good software business, bad legal overhang” credits, bond investors may underestimate how fast enterprise value can compress.

## Hidden-mark-risk thesis
Regulatory clouds often do not move marks all at once. Instead they chip away at:
- buyer universe
- exit multiple confidence
- refinancing appetite
- confidence in long-run franchise durability

## What to watch
- evolution of DOJ / antitrust / state actions
- debt amendments or repricings
- whether legal pressure leads to weaker lender assumptions on recoveries
- any sponsor actions to preserve or reframe value

## Working judgment
- Hidden mark risk: **4 / 5**
- BDC bond relevance: **3 / 5**
- Recovery fragility: **4 / 5**
