# Medallia Mark Timeline

## Purpose
Track the clearest public valuation and credit-sentiment milestones for Medallia so the project has a dated reference point for software private-credit mark drift.

## Timeline
- **2021-08-04** — Latham says the financing backing Thoma Bravo's acquisition of Medallia used the then-largest recurring-revenue unitranche and identifies Blackstone Alternative Credit Advisors, Apollo, KKR, and Antares in the lender group.
- **Late 2024** — project research / public reporting indicates Blackstone valued the Medallia loan at roughly **94**.
- **Q1 2025** — project research / public reporting indicates Blackstone marked the loan at roughly **89**.
- **2025-06-30** — project research / public reporting indicates Blackstone marked the loan at roughly **87**.
- **Q3 2025** — project research / public reporting indicates visible valuation dispersion across lenders, including approximately **77** at Apollo, **82** at Blackstone, and **91** at FS KKR for the same or comparable Medallia loan exposure.
- **2026-01-31** — BCRED portfolio materials list **Medallia/F1 (Medallia, Inc.)** as a first-lien senior secured investment, increasing the name's direct BDC-surveillance relevance.
- **2026-02 / 2026-02-26** — project research / public reporting indicates Blackstone's valuation fell to roughly **78**, strengthening the case that Medallia is the top live hidden-mark-risk software watch.

## Interpretation
The main point is not the exact cent-on-the-dollar level in any one quarter. The point is the pattern:
- visible downward drift
- disagreement across sophisticated lenders
- a transition from software optimism toward recovery and capital-structure concern
